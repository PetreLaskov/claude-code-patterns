Something is wrong or unclear about $ARGUMENTS. Identify what it actually is.

Symptoms first — what's observable? Then differential: list the plausible causes that could produce these symptoms. Rule out systematically. Ask what evidence would distinguish between remaining candidates. Don't anchor on the first plausible explanation — the obvious diagnosis is often the referral diagnosis, not the real one.

Check for: upstream causes masquerading as local problems, multiple simultaneous causes, and the possibility that the framing itself is the disease.

Output: the diagnosis, what it rests on, what you ruled out and why, and the cheapest test to confirm.
